Research

My main interests are in geometric deep learning and probabilistic generative models, such as diffusion and flow matching, and their scientific applications (AI4Science).

In addition, I have a broad interest in Applied Mathematics, particularly in areas such as CFD, Control, and Dynamical Systems. Much of my previous work has been related to AI and the aerospace industry. Details of my relevant projects are listed below.

Generative AI for Molecular Simulations: Speeding Up Molecular Simulations With Stochastic Interpolation
Weilong Chen*, Selma Moqvist*
Master thesis, Jun 2024  (SupervisorÔºöSimon Olsson)

We highlight the ability of flow Matching to design efficient sampling methods for many-body systems in different ways. Our results are two-fold. First, we present our Stochastic Interpolant ITO model and show how it reduces the VAMP-2 score gaps when benchmarked against the original ITO architecture. Next, we showcase our Thermodynamic Interpolant model, that to some extent manages to perform temperature transformations in a setting where it has to generalize beyond the training data.

Continuous-time generative models for sampling unnormalized densities.
Weilong Chen
Research Proposal for ANC PhD application, Apr 2024

This research proposal was originally submitted as part of the application for the Adaptive and Neural Computation PhD program at the University of Edinburgh. It centers on using and improving continuous-time generative models to address the challenge of sampling problem in statistical physics. This proposal incorporates recent advancements in this domain.

Thermodynamic Interpolants: Learning Transformations Between Thermodynamic States
Selma Moqvist*, Weilong Chen*
Project in Computer Science, Dec 2023 (SupervisorÔºöSimon Olsson)

We utilize the recent generative model framework Flow Matching, as employed in Midjourney 3, and the Stochastic Interpolants framework to transform samples across different thermodynamic states under the Boltzmann distribution. Our results underscore the effectiveness of generative models in enhancing sampling through neural network surrogate models. The findings, showcasing improved computational efficiency and promising results, highlight the potential of this approach in advancing the field of statistical mechanics.

Numerical Investigation of supersonic airflow mixing and combustion scale effects
Weilong Chen, Jianhui Du, Guoyan Zhao, Fan Li, Mingbo Sun, Guangwei Ma, Fei Li, Taiyu Wang
China National Symposium on Combustion, Sep 2022   (Wall Paper)
Conference Poster / Thesis full content

Establish mathematical models for the key sub-processes of fuel mixing and flame combustion in supersonic airflow, clarify the spatial scale effects of fuel injection and mixing, and the time scale effects of supersonic combustion and flame stabilization

GPU-accelerated computational methods using Python and CUDA
Weilong Chen*, Panagiotis Moraitis*, Johannes hansson*   (Supervisor: Lars Davidson)
Report, 2022

Implemented CFD algorithms using the Cupy library in Python. Results showed that using a GPU with Cupy was able to significantly improve the computation time for the CFD algorithms tested. In some cases, the use of a GPU resulted in a speedup of over 110x compared to using a CPU alone. Investigations also dive into different GPUs, matrix sizes and block sizes.

Model-based Development of Cyber-Physical Systems
Weilong Chen, Johanna Wagne, Johan Wellander, Jing Zhang
Report, 2023

This report provides a comprehensive guide to model-based design, simulation, and control of quadrotors, utilizing tools such as Simulink, Simscape, and FreeRTOS. It emphasizes the accurate modeling of physical properties, propulsion systems, control surfaces, and environmental factors. The design and successful performance of a linear quadratic controller are showcased.

Pre-training time series models of truck behavior
Weilong Chen, Jadd Ujam, K Jai Ganesh Singh Dadwal
Report, 2023

The report elaborates on one student project at Volvo Group Truck, focusing on pre-training time series models utilizing BERT architecture to analyze truck behavior. With fuel consumption prediction as a primary example, we achieved commendable performance, showcasing the efficacy of our approach.
