Thermodynamic Interpolants: Learning Transformations Between Thermodynamic States
Weilong Chen, Selma Moqvist,
Project in Computer Science & Master thesis, Ongoing   (SupervisorSimon Olsson)

We utilize the recent generative model framework Flow Matching, as employed in Midjourney 3, and the Stochastic Interpolant framework to transform samples across different thermodynamic states under the Boltzmann distribution. Our results underscore the effectiveness of generative models in enhancing sampling through neural network surrogate models. The findings, showcasing improved computational efficiency and promising results, highlight the potential of this approach in advancing the field of statistical mechanics.

Numerical Investigation of supersonic airflow mixing and combustion scale effects
Weilong Chen, Jianhui Du, Guoyan Zhao, Fan Li, Mingbo Sun, Guangwei Ma, Fei Li, Taiyu Wang
China National Symposium on Combustion, Sep 2022   (Wall Paper)
conference page / graduation thesis full content

Establish mathematical models for the key sub-processes of fuel mixing and flame combustion in supersonic airflow, clarify the spatial scale effects of fuel injection and mixing, and the time scale effects of supersonic combustion and flame stabilization

GPU-accelerated computational methods using Python and CUDA
Weilong Chen, Panagiotis Moraitis, Johannes hansson   (Supervisor: Lars Davidson)
Report, 2022

Implemented CFD algorithms using the Cupy library in Python. Results showed that using a GPU with Cupy was able to significantly improve the computation time for the CFD algorithms tested. In some cases, the use of a GPU resulted in a speedup of over 110x compared to using a CPU alone. Investigations also dive into different GPUs, matrix sizes and block sizes.

Model-based Development of Cyber-Physical Systems
Weilong Chen, Johanna Wagne, Johan Wellander, Jing Zhang
Report, 2023

This report provides a comprehensive guide to model-based design, simulation, and control of quadrotors, utilizing tools such as Simulink, Simscape, and FreeRTOS. It emphasizes the accurate modeling of physical properties, propulsion systems, control surfaces, and environmental factors. The design and successful performance of a linear quadratic controller are showcased.
